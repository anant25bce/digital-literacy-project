Task 5 : Cybercrime Awareness Case Study & Prevention Guide

Part A – Case Study:

Phishing Case Study

What Phishing is : Phishing is when bad people pretend to be people you trust like your bank or
college to get you to give them information like your password or bank details. They do this by
sending you emails or taking you to fake websites.

How Phishing happens :

1. The bad person sends you an email that says something like "You have to verify that you paid
your fees or your account will be suspended." They make it look like it is from a place you trust
like the finance office at VIT Bhopal.

2. The email has a logo that looks real and a link to a website that looks like the VITyarthi portal.

3. You click on the link. Enter your college email and password or your UPI details on the fake
website.

4. The bad person gets your information and the fake website says "You could not log in contact
support."

5. Then the bad person uses your information to take your money or get into your account.

Who gets targeted by Phishing : College students like you are the people who get targeted the
most. This is because you do not have a lot of experience and you do things online a lot like using
Google Pay or PhonePe to pay for things.

What happens when you get targeted by Phishing : When you get targeted by Phishing you can
lose a lot of money from your bank account. For example someone could take ₹50,000 from your
account. You can also have problems with your credit score. Someone could use your identity to
do bad things. One student, Ravi lost his semester fees and scholarship because he clicked on a
link. He had to deal with the bank for weeks. It was very stressful, for him. If this happens to you
you should go to cybercrime.gov.in away so you can try to stop the bad people from doing any
more damage.

Part B – Prevention Checklist:

Stay Safe Online Checklist for College Students

•

Just double-check each web site address before clicking on a link. Be sure the link is
spelled correctly. For example do not go to a website like vitbhopa1.com.

•  You should turn on two-factor authentication for your email and other accounts like UPI

apps and college portals. This is also known as Enable 2FA.

•  Do not disclose any personal details and OTP. If a call pretending to be from Paytm or

bank don’t take it lightly and don’t share your otp or details.

•  You should create separate passwords for each account and use the strongest password
options available to you. Make sure you have different passwords for different accounts
and the should be strong , a password is called strong when its length will be more than
12 characters and includes special characters and symbols.

•  Try to avoid using public Wi-Fi when you are doing banking or UPI transactions. It is better

to use your data or the college VPN.

•  Check what permissions you have given to your apps. If you have an app that you do not
use you should take away its permission to access your camera or location. This is called
Check app permissions.

•

If something bad happens you should report it away. You can go to the National Cyber
Crime Portal at cybercrime.gov.in. You can call the Helpline at 1930. This is what we mean
by Report incidents.

•  You should update your phone and apps every week. This helps to fix security problems.

We call this Update phone/apps .

•  Keep an eye on your accounts. Check your UPI transaction history every day to see if there
is anything. You can do this by looking at your passbook. This is known as Log activity.
